This old home of yours has always been the same for as long as you can remember. Empty rooms, empty chairs, and nobody nowadays to visit to ask how you are. No one sends you letters, ever. And yet on your dinner table that day, there it lies: an envelope. No return address.

You feel the familiarity of your dining room feel strange and suffocating. Seeing the letter there makes it so.

Your neck feels cold.

The name of the sender is your childhood friend who went missing ten years ago.

Pick it up
Throw it away
