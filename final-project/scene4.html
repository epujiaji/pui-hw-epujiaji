You grab the phone and dial a string of numbers you thought you’d never call again.

The voice of your best friend’s mother is slower, older, but still very warm. When you said your name, recognition dawns, and it’s apparent from their tone that they’re smiling, and it hurts.

You force yourself to speak.

“Oh, dear! It’s so good to hear from you! It’s been so long. How are you? How is everything?”

“I’m good, I’m good. Now, Mrs—”

“Will you come over for dinner sometime? We should catch up, it’s been ages.”

“Actually, I’m calling to tell you something…”

Her voice darkens.

“What are you talking about?”

“Are you only calling to hurt me?”

“They can’t have been. They’re gone. My child can’t write letters if they’re gone.”

“I'm sorry. I don’t think we should have this conversation.”
